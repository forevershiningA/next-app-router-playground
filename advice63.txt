Why: It connects the UI tab they just clicked to the physical part of the product they are editing.

3. Tighten the Input/Unit Proximity

The Fix: Move the "mm" text closer to the numeric input box.

Why: Currently, there is quite a large gap. In UI design, proximity implies relationship. Keeping them close makes them read as one single value (e.g., "840mm") rather than two separate pieces of information.

4. Finish Selection (Visual Cards?)

The vertical list with descriptions is very clear, but it takes up a lot of vertical space.

Alternative: If you find the panel is getting too long, you could turn these into "Selectable Cards" (small boxes with an icon of the texture and the name below it).

5. Check your Industry Terms

Question: Is the base "Height" actually its Thickness?

In the monument industry, a base usually has Width (left to right), Depth (front to back), and Height/Thickness (how tall it sits off the ground).

If your "Headstone" tab has "Thickness," users might expect the Base tab to have it too. Using consistent names across tabs helps reduce confusion.
