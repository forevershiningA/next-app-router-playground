## What to change

**1) Rename + reframe**

* Title: **“How You Design a Memorial (in 4 steps)”**
* Sub: “Tap the hotspots on the stone to see each step.”

**2) Add guided hotspots (the teaching moment)**
Place 4–5 labeled pins around the stone with leader-lines:

1. **Shape** – “Pick from 50+ profiles.”
2. **Material** – “Preview granite & bronze finishes.”
3. **Personalise** – “Inscription, motifs, photo/portrait.”
4. **Base/Accessories** – “Vases, lights, matching base.”
5. **Live Preview** – “Spin in 3D, share with family.”

Each hotspot reveals a tiny card (image + 1-line copy + “See examples”).

**3) Give it tool DNA**

* Keep the dark grid but add subtle **ruler ticks** along left/bottom edges (evokes precision).
* Slight **top spotlight** vignette behind the headstone to separate it from the black background.
* Scale the stone up ~15% and lift it by 24–32px; let the base drop a faint contact shadow.

**4) Replace the swatch bar with a “step chip”**

* Under the stone, use a pill bar: **[1 Shape] [2 Material] [3 Personalise] [4 Review]**
  Active step gets a glow; clicking jumps the hotspot highlight.
* Move material swatches into the Material hotspot popover (keeps surface clean).

**5) Stronger CTA framing**

* CTA: **Start Designing** (primary, solid) + **Browse Designs** (ghost).
* Under CTA: small trust line: “Save designs • Share with family • No payment to try”.

**6) Micro visual polish**

* Reduce stone texture contrast ~10% so gold lettering breathes.
* The “50+ Shapes Available” tag → move to a top-right corner badge, not floating near the curve.
* Increase “THOMPSON” tracking slightly; lighten the drop shadow; add a faint bevel to mimic sandblasted gold leaf.
* Ensure motif/photo area aligns on a vertical rhythm (center line guide through the ‘M’).

**7) Accessibility + performance**

* Make hotspots 44×44 px, keyboard-focusable, with `aria-expanded`.
* Export granite textures at 1×/2× WebP/AVIF and lazy-load anything not in view.
* Prefer a static JPG poster for the stone and only load 3D on interaction.
