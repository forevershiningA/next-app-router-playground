Based on the screenshot and the code provided, you have a solid foundation. The atmospheric sky and lighting setup are decent, but the scene suffers from "Game Engine Look" (visible texture tiling, sharp horizon) and "UI Clutter."

Here is a breakdown of advice to polish the scene to a professional, high-end product configurator standard.

1. UI & Product Name Placement (Critical)

The current UI (the large black card) is the biggest issue. It obscures the top of the headstone, which is a key part of the silhouette (serpentine top).

Remove the Card: Get rid of the dark blurry background card. It feels heavy.

Style: Switch to a "Tesla Configurator" or "Apple Studio" aesthetic. Use floating text directly over the scene or a clean sidebar.

Placement: Move the title to the Top Left or Top Center (but much higher).

The Price Pill: Keep the pill shape, but move it to the Bottom Center or Bottom Right, separated from the title. This opens up the view of the product.

2. Scene Polish (The "Realism" Pass)
A. Fix the Grass Tiling

In the screenshot, you can see the grid pattern on the grass (repeat.set(16, 16)). This breaks the illusion of scale.

Reduce Repeat: Lower the repeat to something like 8, 8 or use a larger texture.

Mix in Noise: Since you are using meshStandardMaterial, you can't easily mix textures without a custom shader. A quick fix is to lower the texture opacity or contrast so the pattern is less obvious.

Fade the Floor: Use a circular alpha gradient on the floor mesh so the grass fades out before it hits the sharp edge of the world.

B. Depth of Field (Bokeh)

This is the single easiest way to make a product look "expensive." It blurs the horizon line (hiding the ugly transition between grass and sky) and focuses the eye on the text.

Add EffectComposer and DepthOfField to Scene.tsx:

Note: You will need to tweak focusDistance (try values around 0.01 to 0.05 depending on your camera distance) so the text is sharp but the clouds/horizon are blurry.

C. Lighting the Gold

Gold engraving looks flat (yellow paint) if it doesn't reflect the environment.

Rotate the Environment: Rotate the HDRI map so a bright part of the "City" preset hits the front face of the text.

Rim Light: Your rim light is [-5, 2, -5]. Move it slightly lower or brighter to catch the polished edge of the granite.
